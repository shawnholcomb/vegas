2021 LXG Draft
The Post-Covid Pursuit of Liver Poisoning

Dates

The dates for the draft this year are Friday, August 27th - Sunday, August 29th. The draft will be held Saturday afternoon/evening as usual. We would love for everyone to be in Vegas Friday evening so that we can meet up and coordinate plans for the weekend but we understand that schedules can be hectic and ask that everyone do their best. If anyone wants to get to Vegas earlier that is always an option and people can meet up and make lodging arrangements together if they like. We will leave this up to everyone individually to decide.

Flight

As of today, flights from Dallas to Las Vegas range in price from $200 for the least expensive Spirit/Frontier flights up to an average of $350 for major airlines. Keep in mind that the cheapest flight may not include carry-on or luggage costs so there may be additional fees. Average flight time for direct flights is about 3 hours but Vegas is Pacific Time so you will gain two hours on the way there. We are having everyone book their own flights so they can travel to Vegas at whichever dates/times are most convenient. If you want to travel with other league members consider reaching out to to them to coordinate travel.

Hotel

The plan is to stay on the strip. There are a number of hotels in the middle that will put us within walking distance of almost anywhere we will want to go. After getting input from some of the Vegas vetrans and checking prices of nearly twenty hotels we narrowed down the field to the choices below. We will discuss these options in the #league-news channel on Discord and take a vote as we normally do.

There are a few items to discuss with the hotel that differ this year from the way we typically do things. Since we are staying in hotels we need to figure the room situation. After looking into it, hotels don't do block rentals with anything less than ten rooms so this isn't an option for us. We will need three or four rooms depending on whether people want to do two people per room or four (two beds, two per room as a way to save money.) There is also the option of a hybrid (two rooms with double-occupancy, one room with four people.) Because there are multiple options we want to determine which hotel we will all stay at, have everyone decide whom they are lodging with and then individual groups will book their rooms. Once all of the rooms are booked we will reach out to the hotel and see if we are able to get the rooms adjacent, or at least on the same floor. We will reach out to everyone to see what their preferences are and try to help coordinate to make this as seamless as possible but feel free to reach out to other league members that you might want to room with.

Hotels in Vegas include resort fees and taxes so there may be some slight price differences from what is posted below but I tried to calculate everything directly through the hotels websites to be as accurate as possible. A number of these prices include some current discounts and promotions that are valid through mid-June so the sooner we can make a decision the more money we can possibly save. Another note, we did look into booking through third-party groups and the prices aren't considerably lower and there is less opportunity for saving once we are there. Once we decide on a hotel I would also recommend signing up for the rewards program through the hotel. These are free and can offer discounts on the rooms or restaurants, etc. at the hotel, especially once we start gambling in the casinos. Another note, many of the hotels only require about thirty percent of the room cost to make a reservation and the rest is paid once you check in or out so the initial booking isn't actually that expensive. Feel free to reach out with any questions. It would be preferable that we discuss this in Discord so everyone has access to all of the questions and discussion that we cover.

With all that said, on to the hotels!
